Swedish Meatballs

Meatballs with a Scandinavian soul

Köttbullar för alla - Meatballs for all!

Ingredients

  • 1lb ground beef
  • 3.5oz ground pork
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • cups beef broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Ingredients

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef and pork. Use your hands or a wooden spoon to mix until just combined.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, egg, flour, salt, black pepper, nutmeg, and white pepper to the meat mixture. Mix until everything is well incorporated.
  3. Cover the mixture with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. When you're ready to cook the meatballs, preheat your oven t 200°C/400°F.
  5. Use your hands or a spoon to shape the meat mixture into small balls, about 1 1/2 inches (3.8 cm) in diameter. You should end up with around 20-25 meatballs.
  6. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving some space between each meatball to allow for even cooking.
  7. Dot the top of each meatball with butter.
  8. bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they're cooked through and lightly browned.
  9. While the meatballs are baking, heat the beef broth in a large saucepan over medium-low heat
  10. Once the meatballs are done, remove them from the oven and carefully place them into the simmering beef broth
  11. Simmer the meatballs in the broth for about 5 minutes to coat them evenly.
  12. If you want to add an extra layer of creaminess, stir in the heavy cream.
  13. Serve the Swedish Meatballs hot with your choice of sides, such as Lingonberry jam or mashed potatoes!
